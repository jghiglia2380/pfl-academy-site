Overview

This chapter introduces students to the concept of careers versus jobs, helping them understand the difference between short-term employment and long-term career paths. Students will explore their interests, skills, and values to begin the career exploration process, and develop a career path plan.

Learning Objectives

  • Understand the difference between a job and a career
  • Identify personal interests, skills, and values for career exploration
  • Research career paths using appropriate resources
  • Develop SMART career goals for short-term and long-term planning
  • Create a personalized career development plan

Detailed Facilitation Guide

Introduction (10 minutes)

  • Welcome students and introduce the topic of jobs vs. careers
  • Review the learning objectives for the day
  • Ask students to share their current thinking about potential future careers
  • Play the audio introduction or read the opening text aloud
  • Facilitate the opening discussion prompt in pairs or small groups

Jobs vs. Careers Discussion (15 minutes)

  • Present the key differences between jobs and careers
  • Review the comparison table as a class
  • Share the case study of Jordan's Journey, asking students to identify elements that made the marketing positions a career rather than just jobs
  • Discuss how careers often involve progression, skill development, and personal investment
  • Ask students to share examples of careers they're familiar with and what makes them careers

Job or Career Activity (15 minutes)

  • Introduce the "Job or Career?" activity
  • Have students work individually or in pairs to classify each scenario
  • Use the timer to keep students on track
  • Bring the class back together to discuss their classifications
  • For each scenario, ask volunteers to share their classification and reasoning
  • Clarify any misconceptions about what constitutes a job versus a career

Self-Assessment (20 minutes)

  • Explain the importance of self-assessment in career exploration
  • Review the four categories: interests, skills, values, and personal preferences
  • Have students work independently on the self-assessment reflection activity
  • Circulate to provide guidance and ensure students are thinking deeply
  • If time allows, have students share one insight from their self-assessment with a partner

Career Research Resources (20 minutes)

  • Introduce the online career research resources
  • If possible, demonstrate one or two resources on a projector
  • Allow students time to explore at least one resource
  • Based on their self-assessment and research, have students identify 2-3 potential careers that align with their interests, skills, and values
  • Have volunteers share one career they're considering and why it might be a good fit

Career Path Planner (20 minutes)

  • Introduce the Career Path Planner skill builder
  • Explain the concept of short-term, mid-term, and long-term planning
  • Review the five categories for each time frame
  • Introduce the concept of SMART goals (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound)
  • Have students begin working on their Career Path Planner
  • Encourage students to be specific and realistic in their planning

Reflection (10 minutes)

  • Guide students through the reflection questions
  • Have students write brief responses to the questions
  • If time allows, have a few volunteers share key insights
  • Preview Day 2 and the Career Implementation Workshop
  • Remind students to continue thinking about their career interests before the next session

Differentiation Strategies

For Struggling Students

  • Provide simplified self-assessment worksheets with more structure
  • Pair students for the career research activity
  • Offer a more limited set of career options to explore initially
  • Provide examples of completed Career Path Planners
  • Offer additional support during the skill builder activity

For Advanced Students

  • Encourage research into specialized or emerging career fields
  • Suggest creating multiple potential career paths for comparison
  • Assign additional research into educational requirements and opportunities
  • Challenge students to create more detailed SMART goals
  • Have students analyze potential obstacles and strategies

Assessment Opportunities

  • Job or Career Activity: Assess students' understanding of the key differences between jobs and careers
  • Self-Assessment Reflection: Evaluate the thoughtfulness and self-awareness in their responses
  • Career Path Planner: Review for completeness, specificity, and alignment with personal interests, skills, and values
  • Class Participation: Observe engagement in discussions and activities
  • Reflection Questions: Assess depth of understanding and ability to connect concepts to personal experiences

Learning Lab Overview

This Learning Lab allows students to apply their career exploration insights from Day 1 to develop personalized career path plans. Students will create comprehensive career timelines, identify potential obstacles, and design accountability systems.

Learning Lab Objectives

  • Apply career exploration insights to develop a detailed career path plan
  • Create a career timeline with key educational and professional milestones
  • Identify potential obstacles in career paths and develop strategies to overcome them
  • Design an accountability system to track progress toward career goals
  • Integrate career plans into personal financial portfolios

Learning Lab Timeline

Time Activity Description
0:00 - 0:10 Introduction Welcome students, review Day 1 concepts, introduce the Learning Lab structure
0:10 - 0:30 Career Path Timeline Students create visual timelines with educational and professional milestones
0:30 - 0:50 Obstacle Analysis Students identify potential obstacles and develop strategies to overcome them
0:50 - 1:10 Accountability System Students design systems to track progress toward career goals
1:10 - 1:30 Portfolio Integration Students connect career plans to broader financial planning
1:30 - 1:45 Peer Review Students share career plans with peers for feedback
1:45 - 1:50 Conclusion Final reflection and chapter summary

Detailed Facilitation Guide

Introduction (10 minutes)

  • Welcome students to Day 2 and the Career Implementation Workshop
  • Briefly review key concepts from Day 1 (jobs vs. careers, self-assessment, career research)
  • Have students share brief reflections on their Career Path Planners from Day 1
  • Explain the Learning Lab structure and objectives for the day
  • Emphasize that today's activities will build on yesterday's foundation to create a comprehensive career plan

Career Path Timeline (20 minutes)

  • Introduce the concept of a career path timeline
  • Explain how the timeline helps visualize the sequence and timing of career development steps
  • Guide students through the Timeline Builder tool
  • Encourage students to be specific about educational milestones, work experiences, skill development, and other important events
  • Remind students to consider realistic timeframes for each milestone
  • Circulate to provide individualized support and feedback

Obstacle Analysis (20 minutes)

  • Share the case study about Taylor overcoming career obstacles
  • Discuss how identifying obstacles in advance can help prepare for challenges
  • Guide students through the Obstacle Analysis Tool
  • Encourage students to think deeply about potential challenges in their career paths
  • Help students develop specific, actionable strategies for overcoming each obstacle
  • Facilitate the peer obstacle analysis activity, providing structure for feedback

Accountability System (20 minutes)

  • Explain the importance of accountability in achieving long-term goals
  • Review the components of an effective accountability system
  • Guide students through the Accountability System Builder
  • Help students identify appropriate progress tracking methods and check-in schedules
  • Discuss the role of accountability partners and support networks
  • Assist students in developing meaningful rewards and reasonable consequences
  • Facilitate the accountability reflection, connecting to students' past experiences with goal achievement

Portfolio Integration (20 minutes)

  • Explain how career planning connects to broader financial planning
  • Guide students through the salary progression research and analysis
  • Help students estimate educational costs and funding sources
  • Discuss return on investment for education in their chosen fields
  • Assist students in connecting career goals to short-term and long-term financial goals
  • Guide students in assembling all components into a comprehensive portfolio

Peer Review (15 minutes)

  • Organize students into pairs or small groups
  • Establish guidelines for constructive feedback
  • Have students share their career plans with peers
  • Provide prompts for peer feedback (e.g., Is the plan realistic? Are the strategies specific and actionable?)
  • Allow time for students to refine their plans based on feedback

Conclusion (5 minutes)

  • Guide students through the final reflection questions
  • Have students share one key insight or next step
  • Review the chapter summary
  • Emphasize that career planning is an ongoing process
  • Connect this chapter to upcoming financial planning topics

Differentiation Strategies

For Struggling Students

  • Provide templates for each activity component
  • Offer guided examples of completed career plans
  • Pair students for collaborative work
  • Provide more structure for peer feedback
  • Break down complex tasks into smaller steps

For Advanced Students

  • Encourage creation of multiple career path scenarios
  • Challenge students to develop more sophisticated financial projections
  • Suggest research into advanced career development strategies
  • Have students create digital versions of career plans with interactive elements
  • Assign leadership roles in peer review and group activities

Assessment Opportunities

  • Career Timeline: Evaluate for specificity, realism, and alignment with personal interests and skills
  • Obstacle Analysis: Assess thoughtfulness of obstacle identification and effectiveness of proposed strategies
  • Accountability System: Review for completeness and practicality
  • Portfolio Integration: Evaluate ability to connect career planning to financial planning
  • Peer Feedback: Observe quality of feedback provided to others
  • Final Reflection: Assess depth of understanding and ability to articulate next steps

Instructor Support

This section provides additional support for instructors teaching Chapter 1.1: Jobs vs. Careers. Here you'll find teaching strategies, answers to common student questions, extension ideas, and troubleshooting guidance.

Common Student Questions

"What if I don't know what career I want yet?"

It's completely normal not to have your career path figured out yet. The activities in this chapter are designed to help you start exploring possibilities based on your interests, skills, and values. Focus on identifying fields that intrigue you rather than specific job titles. Remember that many successful professionals changed career directions multiple times.

"How realistic do my career goals need to be?"

Your goals should be ambitious but achievable. Consider factors like educational requirements, job market trends, and personal circumstances. It's good to dream big, but also develop a practical plan for getting there. Your career path may include stepping stones that lead to your ultimate goal.

"What if my interests and skills don't match?"

This is common and not necessarily a problem. You might be interested in areas where you can develop skills over time. Alternatively, you might discover career paths that combine your existing skills with your interests in unexpected ways. Many careers allow for growth and specialization as you progress.

Teaching Strategies

Career Professional Panels

Consider inviting professionals from diverse career fields to speak with students. This could be in-person or via video call. Prepare students to ask questions about career paths, education requirements, and day-to-day responsibilities. This provides real-world context for the concepts in the chapter.

Career Path Visualization

Have students create visual representations of career paths using flowcharts, mind maps, or timelines. This helps them see connections between education, skills, and advancement opportunities. Visual learners often benefit from seeing career progression mapped out.

Personal Story Sharing

Share your own career journey or those of other teachers, highlighting changes in direction, obstacles overcome, and how education and experiences built upon each other. This helps students understand that careers often evolve and don't always follow a straight line.

Extension Ideas

Career Field Deep Dive

Have advanced students research a specific career field in depth, including education requirements, salary ranges, job outlook, and day-to-day responsibilities. Students can create presentations or infographics to share with the class.

Career Path Interview Project

Students identify someone working in a field of interest and conduct an interview about their career path. Questions should focus on education, key decisions, obstacles overcome, and advice for someone entering the field.

Career Evolution Research

Students research how specific careers have evolved over the past 20 years and how they might change in the future. This helps students understand the importance of adaptability and continuous learning in long-term career success.

Troubleshooting

Students Struggling with Self-Assessment

Some students may have difficulty identifying their interests, skills, or values. Provide structured prompts or questionnaires to help guide their reflection. Consider having students ask friends or family what they see as the student's strengths and interests.

Limited Access to Career Resources

If internet access is limited or restricted, prepare printed materials from career information websites in advance. Alternatively, create cards or handouts with information about different career fields for students to explore in class.

Career Planning Anxiety

Some students may feel anxious about making career decisions. Emphasize that the goal is exploration, not commitment, and that career plans can and often do change over time. Focus on identifying interests and developing skills that can be valuable in multiple paths.
